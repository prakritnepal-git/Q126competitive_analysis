Q1 26 Earnings Call Highlights

CNXN vs. CDW, NSIT, PLUS

Earnings Call Takeaways

Universal recurring themes across all competitors

  • 1

    AI Structural Deployments: Across all three competitors, AI has moved past the exploratory phase into structural deployments.

  • 2

    Supply Chain Friction: Supply chain friction and memory volatility particularly regarding memory chips and accelerated compute are universal headwinds.

  • 3

    Elevated Backlogs: Historically elevated order backlogs driven by customer demand and supply chain friction.

  • 4

    Efficiency Focus: Prioritizing utilizing AI and internal restructuring to do more with less and drive efficiency.

  • 5

    Cautious H2 Tone: Despite strong results, all three competitors adopted a cautious tone regarding the second half of the year.

CDW

"Geared for growth through AI-first execution"

Financial Pulse
  • Q1 Results: Delivered net sales of $5.7 billion (+9%) and record non-GAAP net income per share of $2.28 (+6.3%).
  • Segment growth: Commercial sector +10% and international +18% offset localized market challenge in Fed and Hi ed.
  • 26 Outlook: Expects GP to grow in the low to mid single digits for full year 2026 driven by elevated backlog.
Operational
  • Geared for Growth: Launched multi-year AI powered modernization targeting quote to cash simplification ($100M-$200M run rate savings by 27/28).
  • GTM alignment: Executing under a newly structured coverage model aligned by cust size and industry verticals.
  • Internal AI: Deployed "CDW Assist super agent" to help sales prioritize opportunities.
AI & Next Gen tech
  • AI-First Strategy: Positioning as "AI first and outcome obsessed" embedding AI directly into core capabilities.
  • GPU aaS: Finalized strategic relationship with Boost Run to deliver AI infrastructure/compute.
  • Enterprise deployment: Secured nearly 8-figure deal with large financial services company for private AI factory.
Market Sentiment
  • Memory Volatility: Customers exhibited buying urgency pulling forward H/W purchases to get ahead of constraints.
  • Infrastructure prioritization: Spending skewed heavily toward networking, server and storage (each up +20%).
  • Lead time normalization: Component lead times are extending but becoming more orderly.

"Scaling through Cloud and AI resilience"

  • Elevated Backlog: Exited Q1 with strong hardware backlog at most elevated level in multiple years.
